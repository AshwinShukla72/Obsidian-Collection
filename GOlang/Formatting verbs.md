
```ad-important

- `%v`: The "default" format. This works for any value, and it formats the value in a default way appropriate for its type.
- `%s`: Formats a string.
- `%d`: Formats an integer as a decimal number.
- `%f`: Formats a floating-point number.
- `%T`: Prints the type of the value.

```

```ad-seealso
- The **`-`** flag left-justifies within the width.
- The **`+`** flag always shows a sign for numbers.
- The **`#`** flag uses alternate formats (e.g., prefixes for octal and hexadecimal).
- The **`0`** flag pads with leading zeros instead of spaces.
```
